Welcome to the AI-Driven Embryo Classification Blog

How AI is being used in developmental biology to predict embryo outcomes early in development.

Model organisms are animals or plants that are used as models for humans during experiments. The point of using model organisms is because animal cells are almost identical throughout species, and testing on humans is unethical. Model organisms are also relatively easy to keep in lab, and have short gestation periods.

AI is now being used to decide which embryos will be most successful in development. Using EtiX-embryos (stem cell-derived structures that mimic early mouse embryogenesis), a deep learning AI named StembryoNet has been trained with hundreds of photos of mouse embryos in different stages of development in order to predict the developmental outcome of the embryos.

This new use of AI technology is revolutionary to medical and biotechnical research because it can show us which embryos will be successful in development in early stages. It can also pinpoint when development goes awry in an embryo, and explain it's reasoning as to why the embryo doesn't appear healthy.
